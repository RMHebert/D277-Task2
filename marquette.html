Marquette

Source: MyMichiganBeach.

Originally settled in 1844, and later incorporated as a village in 1849, and again as a city in 1871, Marquette is the largest city in Michigan's Upper Peninsula. Much of its development is connected as a shipping hub for mines and ores.

Nowadays, Marquette is known for its scenic cityscape, recreational facilities, and abundant natural beauty.

There isn't a common demonym for the people of Marquette. However, being in the Upper Peninsula, they are "Yoopers".
