Grand Rapids

Source: Redfin.

Originally settled in 1826, and later incorporated as a village in 1838, and again as a city in 1850, Grand Rapids began as a trade hub. By the 20th century, Grand Rapids had a well-established industry. It was once famed as the "Furniture City", and was an early hub of the newly-burgeoning automobile industry.

Today, Grand Rapids is known for its support of the arts. It hosts ArtPrize each year, where the works of thousands of artists are displayed and celebrated. It is also known for its craft beer, and has won the title of "Beer City, USA" for the last 10 consecutive years.

People from Grand Rapids are called "Grand Rapidians".
