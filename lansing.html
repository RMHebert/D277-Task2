Lansing

Source: Lansing501.

Originally settled in 1835, and later incorporated in 1859, Lansing was officially declared the state capitol in 1847. The capitol was previously in Detroit, but was moved westward to develop the state and as a defensive measure against the British.

It had a prominent anti-slavery movement in its day, and was a secondary stop along the Underground Railroad.

Today, much of the city's recognizable infrastucture was developed due to industrialization during the rise of now-defunct automobile companies.

People from Lansing are sometimes referred to as "Lansingites" or "Lanstronauts".
